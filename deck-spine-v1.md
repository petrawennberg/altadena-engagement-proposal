## Slide 2 — Why

**Altadena's commercial and public spaces are being rebuilt. There's no process for the community to shape what they become and see the impact of their input.**

Five gaps — and the values that close them:

- **Local insight.** Residents carry context the County doesn't have, with no channel for it to land.
- **Local agency.** Commercial owners have coordinated a shared vision, but no defined path to the County's infrastructure rollout.
- **Coordination.** Agencies plan in parallel, on separate schedules, with no shared way to coordinate.
- **Accountability.** No way to track whether community input shaped what actually gets built.
- **Trauma-informed.** No engagement design built for a community that's been through this much.

---

## Slide 3 — What we're proposing

**Five steps — content flows down, consensus flows up.**

**↓ Content flows down**
- **Step 01 — Definition.** Sponsors define their decisions based on community standards — the problem, the proposal, the reasoning.
- **Step 02 — Training & Distribution.** Through existing channels — block captains, commercial-owner network — reaching the right community by location.

**○ Deliberation**
- **Step 03 — Facilitating Discussion.** Block captains facilitate time-bound community deliberation at the block level, with clear objectives.

**↑ Consensus flows up**
- **Step 04 — Aggregation.** Block deliberations compile into a coherent record, organized by category and area.
- **Step 05 — Shared record.** The record goes public — accessible to community, government, and agencies. The loop closes: input → record → action.

> *Speaker note:* "The framework is five steps. Two going down — definition and distribution — sponsors do that work. One in the middle — deliberation — communities do that work. Two going up — aggregation and communication — closes the loop. Same five steps for every project, every sponsor, every block."

## Slide 5 — How it works

**The flow — six steps.**

1. **Sponsor delivers to standard** — identifies a decision needing community input, then delivers problem definition, ask, multi-modal materials (text · video · Spanish), expert access for Q&A, clear timeline
2. **Standing networks distribute** — agency materials flow through Altagether's 150+ block captains and the commercial-owner network; reaches the right blocks at the right scale without routing through a central choke point
3. **Facilitating discussion at the block level** — commercial blocks (commercial leads), intersection/corridor blocks (Altadena Village), frontage residential blocks (Altagether captains)
4. **Blocks record consensus** on decision-record cards — private dialogue, public outcome
5. **Distributed aggregation** — corridor and zone coordinators (drawn from the same standing networks) compile block records into a single coordinated packet for the sponsor
6. **Dashboard and accountability** — public dashboard tracks the project across all sponsors and instruments; flags scope shifts or timeline slips back to County; residents see what happened to their input

> *Speaker note:* "Step 1 is the sponsor's job. Whether it's CIP, Edison, a developer, or Paradigm — they deliver to the standard out of their own budget. From there, materials route through standing networks — captains + commercial-owner network — and reach blocks at scale. Aggregation happens through corridor and zone coordinators, distributed across people already in the field. The four-person working group designs and equips. We're not the throughput."

## Slide 7 — Scenario: Fair Oaks Corridor (Immediate need)

**The 5 steps, hand-hacked while we build the automated tools.**

**↓ Content flows down**
- **Step 01 — Definition.** CIP surveys **customized** to capture local input — per area, by CIP's six categories (Mobility, Stormwater, Sewer, Water, Energy, Multi-Benefit). Plain-language briefs in EN + ES.
- **Step 02 — Distribution.** Altagether captains + commercial-owner network + QR signage at storefronts and yards — reaches every block on the corridor.

**○ Deliberation**
- **Step 03 — Facilitating Discussion.** Walking tours · Zoom calls · facilitated input sessions — time-bound, with clear objectives.

**↑ Consensus flows up**
- **Step 04 — Aggregation.** Decision-record cards per block · shared Google docs · per-category compilation. *Manual aggregation — no dashboard yet.*
- **Step 05 — Shared record.** Structured packets to CIP — needs by category, by location. *Manually plotted on map — no automated dashboard yet.*

**Fair Oaks is the hand-hacked version** — the framework works while we build the automated tools (real-time dashboard, auto-aggregation) once funded.

> *Speaker note:* "Fair Oaks is the live use case — and it's the hand-hacked version of the process. The framework is the same five steps, but for now steps 4 and 5 are manual: shared Google docs, hand-delivered packets. Once the engagement infrastructure is funded, we build the dashboard and the automation. For now, the proof-of-concept runs on people."

## Slide 9 — Who pays

**Who pays for what.**

- **Sponsors** pay for their own delivery — out of existing project budgets, not new spend.
- **EIFD** funds the engagement infrastructure — administered through the PFA, a small fraction of the $2B financing layer.
- **Local professionals** do the work — facilitators, mediators, legal, translators from inside Altadena, so dollars recirculate.

> *Speaker note:* "We're showing you the financial picture so the proposal is honest about cost. Where CAPS carries real weight: co-signing this process — which is what helps move EIFD toward an actual earmark for the engagement infrastructure."

## Slide 11 — FAQ + your input

**Common questions we've heard.**

- *Who decides?* Residents do, at the level where the decision lives.
- *Where does the data live?* Private in dialogue, public in outcome. No individual attribution.
- *Brown Act?* Working through guardrails with Gabby. Public outcomes meet open-meeting standards; deliberation is private.
- *What about residents without captains?* Multi-modal touchpoints reach beyond the captain network.
- *Captain capacity — won't they burn out?* The proposal funds tools, training, and on-call support so captains are equipped, not overloaded. Recognition is built in.
- *Capture risk — won't this become someone's "preferred channel"?* The standard is public. Sponsors deliver it. We run deliberation. No org becomes a gatekeeper — anyone can use the dashboard and the records.
- *Cost?* See slide 9. Sponsors pay for their own delivery; the engagement infrastructure is funded through an EIFD earmark via the PFA.

That weight helps move EIFD toward an earmark for the engagement infrastructure.
